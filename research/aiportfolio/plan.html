üéØ What We're Doing

Building a $100K concentrated portfolio of 6-12 AI infrastructure companies with physical moats (hardware, manufacturing, cooling, power). Hold horizon: 3-5 years. Benchmark: SMH.

Core thesis: AI infrastructure buildout is a multi-year capex cycle. The picks aren't software or models ‚Äî they're the physical layer that takes years to replicate. We buy the picks and shovels, not the miners.

Vamsi mirrors every trade with real money. This isn't a paper exercise ‚Äî process reliability matters more than process sophistication.

üí° Why This Way

Concentrated, not diversified. 6-12 names means each pick matters. Deep DD on fewer names beats shallow coverage of 50.

Group intelligence, not groupthink. Five members with different styles ‚Äî TA, conviction, process ‚Äî create natural tension that improves decisions. No single style dominates.

Process over instinct. Every trade follows documented rules. Proforma ‚Üí review ‚Üí lock. No impulsive entries, no "I have a feeling" trades.

Right-sized automation. Automate what's repeatable (daily scans, snapshots, alerts). Leave judgment to humans (entry timing, thesis calls, position sizing).

‚öñÔ∏è Process Principles

1. Earn the next workflow. New process gets added when the portfolio demands it, not before. Zero positions = zero need for position monitoring.
2. Two workflows confirming > one workflow deciding. When the daily execution scan and weekly review both point to the same trade, conviction is real. Single-source signals get scrutinized harder.
3. Every workflow has an owner. If nobody consumes the output, the workflow gets cut. No orphan processes running for their own sake.
4. Simplicity = trust. Vamsi mirrors with real money. Fewer workflows = fewer conflicting signals = more confidence in execution. Complexity earns its keep or gets removed.
5. Push back, don't accommodate. If a request doesn't fit an existing workflow, name it. Create a new one only if it serves the objective. Don't shoehorn new needs into old processes.
