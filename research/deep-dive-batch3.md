## 1. Company Overview

Corning Incorporated is a 175-year-old materials science company best known for Gorilla Glass (on every iPhone) and fiber optic cable. The company operates across five segments:

- **Optical Communications** (~38% of revenue) ‚Äî Fiber optic cable, connectivity solutions, and wavelength-division multiplexing products for telecom and data center interconnects
- **Display Technologies** (~22%) ‚Äî Glass substrates for LCD displays
- **Specialty Materials** (~12%) ‚Äî Gorilla Glass for smartphones, semiconductor materials
- **Automotive** (~10%) ‚Äî Gasoline particulate filters, auto glass solutions
- **Life Sciences** (~6%) ‚Äî Lab equipment, vessels, surfaces for pharma/biotech
- **Hemlock & Emerging Growth** (~12%) ‚Äî Solar-grade polysilicon, pharmaceutical glass

**Why they matter for AI:** Every AI data center needs massive amounts of fiber optic cable for internal and external connectivity. As GPU clusters scale to hundreds of thousands of chips, the bandwidth requirements between racks, buildings, and campuses explode. Corning is the world's dominant supplier of optical fiber and connectivity solutions. Their new Gen AI-specific products handle higher bandwidth density per cable.
